## Why Use a QR Code for WhatsApp?

WhatsApp is one of the most popular messaging apps in the world, with over 2 billion users. A QR code that opens a WhatsApp chat eliminates the need for customers to save your number or type a message manually. One scan, and they're chatting with you.

## How to Build a WhatsApp Link

WhatsApp uses the `wa.me` domain for direct chat links. The format is:

```
https://wa.me/COUNTRYCODENUMBER?text=YOUR+MESSAGE
```

**Example:** To open a chat with the number +1 555 123 4567 with the message "Hello, I'd like more info":

```
https://wa.me/15551234567?text=Hello%2C+I%27d+like+more+info
```

### Important Rules

- **Do not include** the `+` sign, dashes, or spaces in the phone number.
- **URL-encode** special characters in the message (`%20` for space, `%2C` for comma).
- The country code is mandatory (e.g., `1` for USA, `44` for UK, `351` for Portugal).

## Step-by-Step: Generate the QR Code

1. Build your WhatsApp link using the format above.
2. Go to [QrGenerate.net](https://qrgenerate.net) and paste the link.
3. Optionally upload your business logo and choose brand colours.
4. Click **Generate QR Code**.
5. Download the PNG and use it on business cards, flyers, or your website.

## Best Practices

- **Add a call to action** near the QR code: "Scan to chat with us on WhatsApp".
- **Test the link** before printing — scan with your own phone first.
- **Use a short, friendly message** as the pre-filled text to reduce friction.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Including the `+` sign in the phone number (use digits only).
- Forgetting the country code.
- Using a personal number instead of a business WhatsApp number.
- Not testing on both iPhone and Android before printing.
